Parts of Speech:

In the English language, there are eight major types of words. They are:

  1. Noun: Nouns are a person, place, thing, or idea. If the noun refers to a specific thing, then it is called a proper noun.
    Ex. happiness, Jack, lunchbox, California
  2. Verb: Verbs show an action done by the subject of a sentence or the state of being of the subject of the sentence.
    Ex. am, is, run, talk, read
  3. Pronoun: Pronouns are words that take the place of nouns.
    Ex. I, she, him, they
  4. Adjective: Adjectives are words that describe nouns or pronouns.
    Ex. red, big, happy, forty-two
  5. Adverb: Adverb are words that describe verbs. Adverbs can describe the where, when, how, or degree of a verb.
    Ex. slowly, well, cheerfully, very
  6. Preposition: Prepositions are words that specify the setting of something.
    Ex. of, before, during, below
  7. Conjuction: Conjuctions are words that join phrases together. There are only seven conjuctions: for, and, not, but, or, yet, and so.
  8. Interjection: Interjections are words that show an emotion. They are usually followed by an exclamation point.
    Ex. Ouch!, Eek!, Hi!, Wahoo!

Modifier and Modifying:

  1. "Modifier" is our informal name of a "dependency".
  2. Dependencies are how words relate, or are dependent, on each other.
  3. "Modifying" is our informal name of a "head".
  4. Heads are the most important word in a phrase.
